Why Angola is the thread
through everything I do

This isn't my polished portfolio — it's the story behind it. How two years on the other side of the Atlantic turned into a business, a language, and a plan.

The short version

I'm a first-generation American from Stockton, California, and the oldest son in my family. At nineteen I left for a 26-month service mission in Angola and São Tomé and Príncipe — where I learned Portuguese, led organizational efforts across Luanda Province, and helped formally open São Tomé to my church.

I came home with a third language and a market I understood from the inside. That's where my electronics business started: buying iPhones here, selling them there.

How a batch actually moves

Every shipment follows the same ordered path — and step 3 is where most of the thinking happens:

  1. Source unlocked devices from trusted U.S. suppliers.
  2. Inspect, grade, and prep each unit for export.
  3. Price the batch — this is the part I model carefully:
    • U.S. cost plus freight and import fees = landed cost
    • Convert to Angolan kwanza at the day's rate
    • Add a target margin that the local market will bear
  4. Ship to Luanda and clear customs.
  5. Hand off to my buyer network and reconcile the numbers.
